# ADA Philosophy & Human Capability Framework

---

# 1. Purpose (WHY)

## Preserving and Strengthening Human Capability in the AI Era

Humans have evolved over thousands of years by questioning, thinking, expressing, and connecting with others.

The ability to think independently, to judge, to express one's own thoughts, and to collaborate with others are not newly invented capabilities of the AI era — they are the essential capabilities of human existence.

However, in an era where AI and automation are rapidly expanding, the uniquely human process of thinking is being challenged in new ways.

As parts of information-seeking, problem-solving, expression, and judgment are delegated to AI, there is a risk that the human process of questioning independently, thinking deeply, expressing directly, and interacting with others may weaken — and screen-centred lifestyles further compound **digital fatigue**.

ADA's purpose is not to create new human capabilities.

It is to preserve and strengthen the thinking and relational capabilities that humans have always possessed, even in the age of AI.

ADA strengthens the following human capabilities:

* The ability to ask questions independently
* The ability to think and make judgments
* The ability to express one's own thoughts
* The ability to understand and embrace different perspectives (**Intellectual Humility**)
* The ability to persuade and collaborate
* The ability to utilise and manage AI

ADA is not a service that replaces AI — it is a **Human Capability Preservation & Development Platform**.

---

# 2. Human Capability Thesis (BELIEF)

## Human value comes from thinking, expression, and connection.

Human value arises not from mere knowledge possession, but from:

* Independent thinking
* Meaningful expression
* Relationships grounded in **Intellectual Humility**
* Collaborative problem-solving
* Judgment and choice

AI is not a substitute for human thinking — it is a tool that extends human thinking capacity.

ADA's core question is not:

> What can AI do?

but rather:

> How can humans maintain their distinctly human capacity for thinking and connection, even in the age of AI?

# 3. Core Values (VALUES)

## Human Agency

The human is the subject of thinking and judgment.

* AI does not think on your behalf.
* Users form their own opinions and conclusions.
* Human choice and responsibility are placed at the centre.

---

## Expression

Thinking grows when it is expressed.

* Communicate your thoughts clearly.
* Make arguments supported by evidence.
* Explain and persuade others.

---

## Connection

Human growth happens through relationships.

* Exchange **Social Cues** through face-to-face conversation.
* Respect and understand others' perspectives grounded in **Intellectual Humility**.
* Understand emotions and atmosphere.

---

## Collaboration

Future capability lies not only in competition, but in collaboration.

* Listen to differing opinions.
* Improve through constructive conflict.
* Build solutions together.

# 4. Design Principles (HOW)

## POF (Person-centred, Offline-first, Face-to-face)

**POF** is the **service design and operating principle** through which ADA preserves and strengthens human capability.

---

## Person-centred

### Human at the Centre

* Humans are the subjects of thinking.
* Design human-centred experiences, not AI-centred ones.
* Prioritise the user's growth and judgment.

---

## Offline-first

### Real-world First

* Prioritise experiences and interactions in the **real-world contexts** where we live our daily lives.
* Place human activity at the centre, not screens.
* Reduce excessive digital dependency to manage **digital fatigue** — AI as a tool expands thinking after human experience.

---

## Face-to-face

### In-person First

Emphasises the **Social Cues** that matter in human relationships:

* Facial expressions
* Emotions
* Atmosphere
* Reactions
* Social signals

# 5. Human Thinking Development Framework

## ICD Framework

### Question (Inquiry) → Thought Formation (Reasoning) → Logical Structuring (Critical Thinking) → Expression & Verification (Deliberation) → Reflection & Growth (Reflection)

**ICD** is the complete process through which human thinking develops.

It is a Human Thinking Development Pipeline of **Question → Thought Formation → Logical Structuring → Expression & Verification → Reflection & Growth**.

---

## I - Inquiry

### Ask Better Questions

The capacity to question

Goals:

* Form better questions
* Discover problems
* Uncover hidden assumptions
* Explore diverse perspectives

---

### Reasoning

#### Develop Thinking

The stage of forming thoughts

Goals:

* Connect information
* Analyse causes
* Form hypotheses
* Develop one's own perspective

---

### Product

#### LogicPilot

##### Inquiry, Reasoning & Critical Thinking Foundation Engine

LogicPilot handles the early thinking process in ICD.

##### Inquiry

* Question generation
* Problem definition
* Perspective expansion

##### Reasoning

* Connecting thoughts
* Maturing ideas
* Developing thinking

##### Critical Thinking Foundation

Case Draft:

* Situation analysis
* Distinguishing fact from opinion
* Identifying core issues
* Stakeholder analysis
* Comparing options
* Constructing grounds for judgment

Goal:

> The ability to form and validate thinking before expressing it

---

## C - Critical Thinking

### Structure & Validate Thinking

Critical Thinking is the core capability developed throughout the entire ICD process.

In LogicPilot: **Analytical Critical Thinking**

In Motion Game: **Argumentative Critical Thinking**

are learned and experienced.

---

### Product

#### Motion Game

#### Argumentative Critical Thinking Training Engine

Motion Game is an experiential environment for transforming thoughts into arguable structures.

Experience:

* Understanding the Motion
* Setting Definitions
* Setting Scope
* Constructing Arguments
* Anticipating Counterarguments
* Reviewing Burden of Proof

Goal:

> The ability to shape thinking into a logical argument structure

---

## D - Deliberation

### Express & Test Thinking

The stage of expressing and verifying thinking within real human relationships.

Goals:

* Express opinions
* Understand the other party's perspective
* Respond to counterarguments
* Persuade
* Collaborative judgment

---

### Product

#### Coda

##### Human Debate Practice Engine

Coda is not simply a speaking practice tool.

It is a process of verifying and improving thinking through real human interaction.

Learning and experience: **Claim → Evidence → Counterargument → Rebuttal → Deliberation**

---

#### Red Team Mode

##### Critical Thinking Stress Test

Red Team is not about defeating the opponent.

Purpose:

* Discover hidden assumptions
* Explore logical weaknesses
* Examine opposing perspectives
* Improve thinking

Core questions:

"What are the reasons my thinking could be wrong?" (**Intellectual Humility**)

"What is the strongest counterargument?"

"Are there any missing perspectives?"

---

## Reflection

### Improve Thinking

After the experience, growth comes through asking:

* What did I learn?
* How has my thinking changed?
* What is my next question?
